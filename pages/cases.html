UX Design Institute · Oisín
Professional Certificate in User Research
Challenge

The UX Design Institute required development of a comprehensive online course teaching user research methodologies to aspiring and early-career UX professionals. The programme needed to translate SME expertise into engaging video-based instruction, support learners in completing project work and a final exam, and maintain academic rigour while remaining accessible to learners at different experience levels.

Role

Instructional Design Lead and Content Strategist. Managed SME coordination, shaped instructional structure, and led development of video lessons, assignments, and assessment materials.

Actions Taken
  • Managed and aligned contributions from three subject matter experts across different UX disciplines
  • Translated SME knowledge into structured video lesson scripts and storyboards
  • Collaborated with video editors and designers to produce polished instructional content
  • Designed homework assignments and practical exercises supporting applied research skills
  • Authored content addressing emerging practices, including AI applications in user research
  • Engaged directly with accreditation stakeholders at Glasgow Caledonian University
Results & Impact
  • Delivered a rigorous, engaging, and accredited course suitable for diverse learner levels
  • Enabled learners to confidently apply research methods through structured instruction
  • Strengthened the institute's professional, credit-rated course portfolio

Oxygen Advantage · Katherine
Advanced Instructor Course Redesign
Challenge

The Advanced Instructor course lacked instructional cohesion and followed a "kitchen sink" structure — content had been added without strategic sequencing. The course required restructuring, improved learner flow, alignment with CPD accreditation standards, and a more evergreen modular structure.

Role

Learning Experience Strategist, Course Architect, and LMS Lead. Led research, instructional redesign, accreditation alignment, and technical implementation.

Actions Taken
  • Conducted learner interviews to identify strengths, gaps, and cognitive load challenges
  • Reviewed CPD accreditation standards and competitor offerings to benchmark quality
  • Redesigned the course into a modular progression model
  • Integrated structured learning objectives, project rubrics, guided discussions, and a resource library
  • Refined video lessons in collaboration with a professional editor
  • Managed LMS restructuring and student migration
Results & Impact
  • Transformed an unstructured content repository into a cohesive, pedagogically sound programme
  • Reduced learner cognitive load and improved course navigation
  • Positioned the course for CPD accreditation readiness
  • Established scalable instructional systems for future programme development
